# What Actualisation Group Actually Does — Deep Explanation

A structured breakdown of their business based on their public site (actualisation.ai) and About/Solutions/Industries pages.

---

## 1. Who they are (facts)

- **Founded:** 2015  
- **Positioning:** One of the largest privately owned AI companies in the APAC region.  
- **Size:** 120+ people, 4 locations (Australia, Europe, Asia; they also mention Canada and India on the main page).  
- **Head office:** Newstead QLD, Australia.  
- **Core claim:** They “do the maths and code that powers **Private AI**” — designing, training, and deploying solutions using **clients’ private data** (i.e. not just plugging into public ChatGPT; they work with enterprise data on the client’s side).

So at the highest level: **they are an enterprise AI services company** that builds custom AI infrastructure and agents for other companies, using those companies’ own data.

---

## 2. The central concept: “AI Factory”

They use **“AI Factory”** as the name for what they build at the heart of a client’s business.

**In plain language:**

- An **AI Factory** is a **central system** that:
  - Holds and organises the company’s **data** (structured and unstructured).
  - Connects that data to **people, processes, platforms and products**.
  - Provides the foundation so the company can run **multiple AI agents** and other AI capabilities in a coordinated way.

So it’s not “one chatbot”. It’s the **platform/layer** that makes it possible to:
- Ingest and store company data in a way AI can use.
- Train or fine-tune models, or use LLMs safely over that data.
- Deploy many different “agentic” or automated solutions (scheduling, coaching, documents, voice, etc.) that all sit on top of this same foundation.

**In their words (Solutions page):**  
*“We specialise in helping companies create robust AI Factories, offering a consolidated repository for structured and unstructured data. We design scalable architectures, implement data ingestion pipelines, and ensure data quality and security measures are in place. We empower organisations to extract valuable insights using LLMs and drive informed decision-making by tailoring AI Factories to specific business needs.”*

So what they **actually do** under “AI Factories” is:

1. **Design and build** a central data/architecture layer for the client.  
2. **Consolidate** the client’s data (repositories, pipelines, quality, security).  
3. **Enable** LLM-based insights and decision-making on that data.  
4. **Tailor** it to the client’s business (industry, use cases, compliance).

That’s the “brain” or “core” they keep referring to: **the data and architecture layer that makes the rest of their solutions possible.**

---

## 3. What they deliver (solutions = products/services)

Beyond the Factory itself, they sell **concrete solutions** that sit on top of it or alongside it. These are what they “actually do” day to day for clients.

| What they call it | What it actually is (in practice) |
|-------------------|-----------------------------------|
| **AI Factories** | Design and build the central data platform: ingestion, storage, quality, security, and LLM-ready access. The “brain” that other solutions use. |
| **Agentic AI** | Design, build and deploy **custom AI agents** that automate workflows and integrate with the client’s systems. Delivered with security, compliance and scalability in mind. |
| **AI Model Training** | Train models on the client’s data (large datasets, real-world outcomes). Use feedback loops so the AI improves over time. So: custom models or fine-tuned models for the client’s domain. |
| **Corporate Memory Retention** | Capture company knowledge (how people work, decisions, experience) and store it in a form AI can use (e.g. vectors). Use it for retrieval, coaching, onboarding, and reducing “corporate memory loss” when people leave. |
| **AI Scheduler** | Rostering/scheduling product. Uses AI on historical data, availability, workload, skills, labour rules and priorities to generate schedules and allocate resources. |
| **VoiceAI** | **Phone call automation** tailored to the client. Uses the client’s data and domain knowledge so conversations are relevant. Integrates with their existing software. |
| **Automated Coaching** | **Real-time feedback** for staff based on actions and performance. AI analyses data from multiple sources and gives personalised coaching so people improve over time. |
| **Cost Optimisation (Procurement)** | Use AI on historical data, market trends and cost factors to suggest procurement and pricing decisions. Aim: cost savings and better bottom line. |
| **Predictive Maintenance** | Use AI on sensor data and maintenance history to **predict failures** and plan maintenance in advance. Goal: less unplanned downtime, lower maintenance cost. |
| **Job Optimisation** | Use AI to match **people to jobs** (skills, performance, business needs). Workforce management and allocation. |
| **Hunter** | **AI lead prospecting**: find sales leads from large data sources, aligned to the client’s goals. Sales/outreach automation. |
| **Document Intelligence** | **Document lifecycle** with AI: extraction, classification, validation, integration. More accurate and faster than manual, so the business can make data-driven decisions. |
| **Software Integration** | Help **integrate** the client’s software stack so systems talk to each other and the AI/Factory can use the right data. |
| **Machine Vision** | **Visual automation**: e.g. quality control, object recognition, real-time data from images. For manufacturing/operations. |
| **Automated Finance** | **Finance automation**: quotes, invoices, reconciliation using AI, plugged into existing software, customised to how the client works. |

So “what they actually do” at the **solution level** is: they **design, build and deploy** these kinds of systems for enterprises. Some are productised (e.g. “AI Scheduler”, “VoiceAI”), others are more custom builds using the same ideas (e.g. custom Agentic AI, Document Intelligence). The **AI Factory** is what ties data and security together; the **solutions** are the specific use cases they implement.

## 4. How they work (delivery model)

They don’t just sell software off the shelf. From their copy:

- They **design** (architecture, use cases, data strategy).  
- They **train** (models on client data).  
- They **deploy** (solutions into the client’s environment).  
- They use **clients’ private data** (Private AI), so deployment is in the client’s systems or controlled cloud, not just “ChatGPT in the browser”.  
- They emphasise **security, compliance, scalability** and **measurable impact**.

So the **delivery model** is: **consulting + build + (where applicable) productised solutions**. The client gets a combination of:

1. **Strategy and design** (what the AI Factory and agents should do).  
2. **Implementation** (data pipelines, models, integrations, agents).  
3. **Concrete solutions** (scheduling, voice, coaching, documents, etc.) that run on that foundation.

## 6. Why they say they exist (positioning)

- **Problem they see:** Companies see AI’s potential but struggle with **how** to implement it (lack of expertise, resources, understanding).  
- **What they do about it:** They remove the “robot” work from humans and give “more time and space” by building the AI Factory and agentic solutions.  
- **Belief:** “Every company in the world will eventually have an AI Factory at their core, housing multiple Agentic AI teams.”  
- **Mission:** “We believe in the freedom to be more human” — i.e. AI does the heavy lifting so people can focus on higher-value work.

So in one sentence: **they exist to design, build and run the “AI core” (Factory + agents + solutions) for enterprises that don’t have the in-house capability to do it themselves.**
